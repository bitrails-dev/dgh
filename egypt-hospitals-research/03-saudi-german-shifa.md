SGH-Cairo is the **first hospital of the Saudi German Health group located in Africa**. It is one of the major tertiary care hospitals in Cairo and Egypt, offering all specialties, sub-specialties, critical care, and management of complicated cases at international standards. The hospital launched the **"Cure in Egypt"** program targeting international medical tourists with all-inclusive healthcare packages.

SGH-Cairo is also the **first and only healthcare facility in Africa to become a member of the Mayo Clinic Care Network (MCCN)**. As of 2025, Saudi German Health has become the largest Mayo Clinic Care Network member group in the Arab region.

The group operates an **International Visiting Professor Program (IVP)**, bringing globally recognized physicians and surgeons to work alongside resident doctors, enabling continual learning and enhancement of treatments like transplant surgery and brachial plexus surgery.

### Key Insights

1. **SGH-Cairo has a stronger international profile** due to its Mayo Clinic Care Network membership, multiple international accreditations (JCI, Accreditation Canada Diamond Level, TEMOS), and dedicated health tourism portal with published pricing for major procedures. It is better positioned for medical tourism.

2. **Shifa has broader departmental coverage** (38+ departments vs. 27 at SGH) and significantly higher outpatient volume (~18,500/month). Its GAHAR 100% compliance achievement is notable as a first for Egyptian private sector.

3. **Both hospitals are at ~300 beds**, positioning them as large private tertiary care facilities in Cairo. However, SGH has the backing of a much larger group (13+ hospitals across MENA) while Shifa is an independent Egyptian group with IFC/World Bank institutional backing.

4. **Shifa's oncology capabilities are advanced** with the Gamma Knife Esprit (first in Africa's private sector) and Elekta strategic partnership, making it a standout for neuro-oncology. SGH counters with Mayo Clinic e-consultation access for complex radiotherapy cases.

5. **Digital presence diverges significantly**: SGH-Cairo has a polished, modern health tourism website with transparent pricing, virtual tours, and multilingual support. Shifa's main website (shifaegypt.com) is under construction, with the legacy site (old.shifaegypt.com) serving as the active web presence.

6. **Insurance and government partnerships**: Shifa has a more publicly visible network of government and petroleum-sector partners (Central Bank of Egypt, Ministry of Justice, Egyptian Parliament, EGPC, etc.), while SGH's insurance coordination is positioned more for international patients via Bupa Global recognition.

7. **Pricing transparency**: SGH-Cairo publishes package prices (Bone Marrow Transplant from $13,000, Liver Transplant from $40,700, Kidney Transplant from $12,000, Hip/Knee Arthroplasty from $8,220-$9,200). Shifa does not publish pricing but emphasizes itemized, transparent billing.

8. **Growth trajectory**: Both are expanding. SGH Group opens approximately one new hospital per year (16% annual growth). Shifa's employee base grew 29.4% YoY and it is actively hiring across departments. Shifa's new website under construction suggests a brand refresh is underway.

### Strategic Observations for EHR Implementation

- **SGH-Cairo already uses secured digital EMR**, indicating some digital infrastructure maturity
- **Shifa's separate billing policy** (itemized per test/procedure) suggests a billing system that needs granular charge capture
- Both hospitals handle high outpatient volumes (Shifa: ~18,500/month), requiring robust OPD management modules
- Both serve international patients, requiring multilingual support and insurance coordination features
- The petroleum-sector and government partnerships (especially at Shifa) suggest complex corporate billing and pre-authorization workflows
