## 1. Ain Shams University Hospitals

### Overview
- **Website:** https://hospitals.asu.edu.eg/
- **Established:** 1928 (Demerdash Hospital, the first)
- **Location:** Abbasia, Cairo
- **Type:** Public / University Teaching Hospital
- **Affiliation:** Ain Shams University Faculty of Medicine (est. 1947)

Ain Shams University Hospitals comprise a medical city of 8 hospitals and multiple centers, making it one of the oldest and largest hospital complexes in Cairo. The university is constructing a new international-standard Medical City at a cost of 10 billion EGP under presidential directives.

### Hospitals & Units

| Hospital | Key Details |
|---------|-------------|
| **El Demerdash Hospital** | Est. 1928, donation from Abdel Rahim Demerdash Pasha. Oldest hospital in the complex. Surgical departments. |
| **Ain Shams Specialized Hospital** | Cardiothoracic surgery, cardiology, vascular, oncology, organ transplantation, orthopedics. 40+ specialties. |
| **Children's Hospital** | Est. 1941. Old building for ER/outpatient/general pediatrics. New building (2017) for specialized units: heart/chest, neurology, hematology, oncology, GI, liver, immunology, diabetes/endocrine. |
| **Obstetrics & Gynecology Hospital** | Maternity and women's health |
| **Emergency Hospital** | New construction, being built under Medical City project. Agreement with US medical centers for management. |
| **Obour Hospital** | General hospital serving Obour area |
| **Heart & Vascular Surgery Hospital** | Cardiac surgery center |
| **Elderly Hospital** | First geriatric hospital in the Middle East |

### Centers & Units
- Psychiatric Center
- Clinical Toxicology Center
- Ain Shams Medical Research Center (MASRI)
- Simulation Center
- Endoscopy Center
- Oncology and Nuclear Medicine Department
- Bone Marrow Transplant Unit
- Laser Surgery Unit (Demerdash)
- Schistosomiasis and Viral Hepatology Research Unit
- Artificial Kidney Unit

### Website Sections & Features
- **URL:** hospitals.asu.edu.eg
- **Sections:** Home, Services, Centers & Units, Statistics, News & Events, Contact
- **Services listed:** Outpatient Clinics, Exeera System (online booking), Diagnostic Radiology, External Laboratory
- **Language:** Arabic + English
- **Digital:** Telemedicine/Virtual Clinics project (sponsored by Sanofi Egypt, announced Feb 2025)

### Fee Structure
- Free treatment for Egyptian citizens (public hospital)
- Specialized units may have nominal fees
- University staff and students receive integrated healthcare
- Exeera system for online appointment booking

### Key Statistics
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Annual Medical Visits | 775,000+ |
| Total Beds | 2,300+ |
| Physicians | 5,000+ |
| Hospitals | 8 |
| Centers & Units | 6+ |
| Libraries | 8 |

### Digital Transformation
- **Medical City Project:** Complete redevelopment at 10 billion EGP cost, including new Emergency Hospital, Oncology Hospital, Eye Hospital, Children's Surgery Hospital, Clinics Building, Central Pharmacy
- **Exeera System:** Online appointment booking
- **Telemedicine:** Virtual Clinics project launched with Sanofi Egypt sponsorship
- **Digital Smart Services Portal:** Available on university website
- **Virtual Tour:** Available on ASU website

### Contact Information
- **Address:** Abbasia, Cairo, Egypt
- **Website:** https://hospitals.asu.edu.eg/
- **Faculty:** https://med.asu.edu.eg/
- **University:** https://www.asu.edu.eg/

### Analysis
- **Strengths:** Largest teaching hospital complex in Cairo after Kasr Al-Ainy; pioneering Medical City redevelopment; first elderly hospital in MENA; active digital transformation with virtual clinics
- **Weaknesses:** Website is basic compared to private hospitals; limited patient-facing digital tools; no online results portal visible
- **Key Differentiator:** Scale (2,300+ beds, 8 hospitals) and the ongoing 10B EGP Medical City transformation

## 2. Cairo University Hospitals (Kasr Al-Ainy)

### Overview
- **Website:** https://cu.edu.eg/ (university) | https://medicine.cu.edu.eg/ (faculty)
- **Established:** 1827 (Kasr Al-Ainy medical school), 1837 (hospital)
- **Location:** Kasr Al-Ainy Street, Cairo
- **Type:** Public / University Teaching Hospital
- **Affiliation:** Cairo University Faculty of Medicine

The oldest and largest educational and medical hospital complex in Egypt and the Middle East. Kasr Al-Ainy is considered the citadel of medicine in the Arab region. March 11, 1827 (founding date) is celebrated as the annual festival for the Egyptian medical profession.

### Hospitals & Units

| Hospital | Key Details |
|---------|-------------|
| **El Qasr El Ainy New Educational Hospital** | 1,200 beds capacity, partially inaugurated 1955, modern construction replacing the original 1837 building (demolition decree 1977) |
| **Al Manial University Hospital** | 1,794 beds, 36 departments, 36 operation theatres, 1,422 doctors, 235 pharmacists, 3,000+ staff. Divided into: Al Manial Al Bahari, Al Manial Al Kibli, Operations Unit |
| **Obstetrics & Gynecology Hospital** | Maternity and women's health |
| **Internal Medicine Hospital** | General medicine departments |
| **Pediatrics New Academic Hospital** | Children's hospital |
| **External Clinic** | Outpatient services |

### Services & Specialties
All major medical and surgical specialties including:
- Surgery (multiple departments: 5, 6, 24, 25, 27, 28, 29, 30)
- Internal Medicine (departments: 4, 9, 12, 14, 15, 16, 19, 20, 31)
- Cardiac and Chest Surgery (Dept. 24)
- Neurosurgery (Dept. 26)
- Cardiac and Eye Diseases (Depts. 12, 14, 16)
- Neurology (Depts. 4, 15)
- Psychiatry
- Chest Diseases
- OB/GYN
- ENT
- Orthopedics (Depts. 7, 20)
- Urology
- Accident Reception & Central Radiology

### Website Sections & Features
- **URLs:** cu.edu.eg (main university), medicine.cu.edu.eg (faculty of medicine)
- **Sections:** Home, Hospitals & Institutes, E-Services, Research, Publications
- **Language:** English + Arabic
- **Limitations:** No dedicated hospital booking system visible; website is primarily informational/academic

### Fee Structure
- Free treatment for Egyptian citizens
- March 11 celebrated as Egyptian Medicine Day
- Teaching hospital serving all economic segments

### Key Statistics
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total Beds | 5,200+ |
| Beds (Al Manial alone) | 1,794 |
| Departments (Al Manial) | 36 |
| Operation Theatres | 36 (Al Manial) |
| Doctors (Al Manial) | 1,422 |
| Pharmacists (Al Manial) | 235 |

### Digital Transformation
- Basic university website infrastructure
- No visible patient portal or online booking system
- E-services available through university portal
- Primarily academic/research focused online presence

### Contact Information
- **Address:** Kasr Al-Ainy Street, Cairo, Egypt
- **University:** https://cu.edu.eg/
- **Faculty:** https://medicine.cu.edu.eg/
- **Hospital Page:** https://cu.edu.eg/page.php?pg=contentFront%2FSubSectionData.php&SubSectionId=199

### Analysis
- **Strengths:** Historical prestige (oldest medical institution in Egypt/Middle East); massive scale (5,200+ beds); supplies the world with skilled physicians
- **Weaknesses:** Very basic web presence; no patient-facing digital tools; website is academic rather than patient-centered
- **Key Differentiator:** Heritage and scale - the symbolic heart of Egyptian medicine

## 3. Mansoura University Hospitals

- **Location:** 60 El Gomhoureya Street, Mansoura, Dakahlia, Egypt

## 6. National Cancer Institute (NCI)

### Overview
- **Website:** http://www.nci.cu.edu.eg/ (Cairo University affiliated)
- **Established:** 1960s
- **Location:** Kasr Al-Ainy, Cairo
- **Type:** Public / Specialized Cancer Hospital
- **Affiliation:** Cairo University

Egypt's primary cancer treatment and research center. The NCI was the catalyst for the creation of Hospital 57357 when its pediatric oncology capacity became overwhelmed.

### Services & Specialties
- Medical Oncology
- Surgical Oncology
- Radiation Therapy
- Clinical Pathology
- Cancer Research
- Pediatric Oncology (until 57357 took over much of this)

### Fee Structure
- Free/subsidized treatment for Egyptian citizens
- Government-funded

### Contact Information
- **Website:** http://www.nci.cu.edu.eg/
- **Location:** Kasr Al-Ainy, Cairo

### Analysis
- **Strengths:** National reference center for cancer; historical importance; research institution
- **Weaknesses:** Very limited web presence; overwhelmed capacity led to creation of 57357
- **Key Differentiator:** Egypt's original and primary cancer research and treatment institution

## Comparative Analysis

### Scale Comparison

### Digital Maturity Comparison

| Hospital | Website Quality | Online Booking | Patient Portal | Telemedicine | Mobile App |
|----------|----------------|----------------|----------------|--------------|------------|
| Ain Shams | Medium | Yes (Exeera) | No | Yes (Virtual Clinics) | No |
| Kasr Al-Ainy | Low | No | No | No | No |
| Mansoura | Low-Medium | No | No | No | No |
| Alexandria | Low | No | No | No | No |
| Assiut | Low | No | No | No | No |
| NCI | Low | No | No | No | No |

### Key Takeaways

1. **Massive scale but minimal digital presence:** University hospitals serve millions annually but have rudimentary websites compared to private hospitals.

2. **Ain Shams leads digital transformation:** The only university hospital with an online booking system (Exeera) and active telemedicine program. The 10B EGP Medical City project is the most ambitious public hospital redevelopment in Egypt.

3. **Kasr Al-Ainy is the heritage institution:** Oldest and largest at 5,200+ beds, but has almost no patient-facing digital tools despite its prestige.

4. **Mansoura's Urology/Nephrology Center is world-class:** This single specialty center has put Mansoura on the global medical map.

5. **Geographic coverage is critical:** Assiut serves all of Upper Egypt with no comparable alternative, making it strategically essential despite limited resources.

6. **Funding model:** All university hospitals are free/subsidized for Egyptian citizens, funded by the government. This contrasts sharply with the 5-6 digit EGP bills at private hospitals.

7. **Medical City trend:** Ain Shams' 10B EGP Medical City project represents a new model of government investment in public hospital infrastructure, potentially setting a template for other universities.
