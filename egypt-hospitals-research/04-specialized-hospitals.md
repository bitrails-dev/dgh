# Children's Cancer Hospital Egypt 57357

## Overview

Children's Cancer Hospital Egypt 57357 (CCHE) is the largest pediatric oncology hospital in the world by capacity and the first comprehensive children's cancer hospital in the Middle East and North Africa region. Founded in 2007 through a massive public donation campaign, the hospital operates as a non-profit, donation-funded institution providing free cancer treatment to all children regardless of financial status.

- **Founded:** 2007
- **Type:** Non-profit, donation-funded (free treatment for all patients)
- **Location:** 1 Seket Al-Wusta Street, El Mataria, Cairo, Egypt
- **Beds:** 300-386 beds
- **Hotline:** 19057
- **Website:** https://www.57357.org/
- **Annual Operating Budget:** Funded entirely through donations (domestic and international)

The hospital was conceived after a 1998 T.V. appeal by Egyptian journalist Dr. Sherif Abouelnaga raised public awareness about the lack of specialized pediatric cancer care in Egypt. Construction was funded by millions of small donations from Egyptian citizens, with the hospital name "57357" originating from the bank account number used for the fundraising campaign.

## Services & Specialties

The hospital operates as a comprehensive cancer center with 16+ clinical departments:

**Oncology Services:**
- Pediatric Medical Oncology
- Pediatric Surgical Oncology
- Radiation Oncology (LINAC-based)
- Hematology / Hemato-Oncology
- Bone Marrow Transplant (BMT) Unit
- Immunotherapy and Targeted Therapy
- Clinical Trials Unit

**Surgical Specialties:**
- Neurosurgery (brain tumor resection)
- Orthopedic Oncology Surgery
- Ophthalmic Oncology (retinoblastoma)
- ENT Surgical Oncology
- General Pediatric Surgery

**Diagnostic & Support Services:**
- Clinical Pathology and Laboratory Medicine
- Radiology (MRI, CT, PET-CT, Ultrasound)
- Nuclear Medicine
- Clinical Pharmacy
- Nutrition Therapy
- Physiotherapy and Rehabilitation
- Psychology and Psychiatry

- Pain Management

**Research & Education:**
- Molecular Oncology Research

- Medical Education and Training Programs
- Nursing Education Institute

## Analysis

Hospital 57357 stands as a unique institution globally -- a world-class pediatric cancer center built and sustained entirely through public donations. Its strengths include:

1. **Scale and Impact:** Treating nearly 100,000 children since inception with survival rates approaching developed-world benchmarks. The hospital has fundamentally transformed pediatric oncology outcomes in Egypt.

2. **Digital Leadership:** As a fully Cerner-powered paperless hospital, 57357 is among the most digitally mature hospitals in Egypt. Its donation platform, patient portal, and telemedicine capabilities are state-of-the-art.

3. **Research Excellence:** The hospital's investment in genomics, proteomics, and clinical trials positions it as a regional research hub, not just a treatment center.

4. **Accreditation Breadth:** Holding JCI, GAHAR, ASHP, and ANCC accreditations simultaneously is rare globally and unprecedented in Egypt.

5. **Website Quality:** The hospital's website is well-structured with clear donation pathways, transparency through annual reports, and comprehensive department information.

6. **Model Transferability:** The donation-funded model could be studied as a template for other specialized healthcare institutions in developing countries, though replicating the cultural momentum that built 57357 would be challenging.

## Analysis

Magrabi is the dominant force in specialized eye care across the Middle East. Key observations:

1. **Brand Legacy:** Nearly a century of continuous operation gives Magrabi unmatched brand trust in the region. The "Magrabi" name is essentially synonymous with eye care in Egypt and Saudi Arabia.

2. **Subspecialization Depth:** Unlike general hospitals that offer eye care as one department among many, Magrabi's entire model is built around deep ophthalmic subspecialization, allowing for expertise concentration that general hospitals cannot match.

3. **Geographic Reach:** The multi-country presence creates referral networks and economies of scale for equipment procurement and talent recruitment.

4. **Website and Digital:** The website is modern and functional with real booking capabilities, though the SPA (single-page application) architecture can present content loading challenges for some users. The mobile app adds convenience.

5. **Pricing Transparency:** A notable gap -- Magrabi does not publish pricing on its website, requiring patients to inquire directly. This contrasts with increasing global trends toward price transparency in healthcare.

6. **Egypt Branch Network:** The Cairo-centric branch network (5 of 7 locations in Greater Cairo) leaves significant gaps in Upper Egypt, the Delta, and the Canal Zone, representing expansion opportunities.

## Analysis

International Eye Hospital presents a differentiated market position within Egyptian ophthalmology. Key observations:

1. **Geographic Spread:** IEH has the widest geographic coverage among Egypt's private eye hospitals, with branches spanning from Luxor in the south to New Damietta in the north. This is a significant competitive advantage for reaching underserved populations.

2. **24-Hour Emergency:** Being the only Egyptian eye hospital with 24/7 emergency service is a powerful differentiator that addresses a genuine gap in the market. Eye emergencies (trauma, acute glaucoma, retinal detachment) require immediate attention that general hospital emergency departments may not be equipped to provide.

3. **Upper Egypt Presence:** Branches in Luxor, Beni Sweif, and Fayoum serve populations with historically limited access to specialized eye care. These locations also support medical tourism (especially Luxor).

4. **Branch Quality vs. Quantity:** While IEH has the most branches, some locations (particularly outside Cairo) may have more limited service scope compared to the main Dokki hospital. The 72 beds distributed across 7+ branches means smaller facility sizes per location.

5. **Kid Eyes Sub-Brand:** The dedicated pediatric eye care branch under a separate "Kid Eyes" brand shows recognition of the specialized needs of pediatric ophthalmology patients.

6. **Website and Digital:** The website is functional but more basic compared to competitors like Magrabi. There is room for improvement in digital patient engagement tools, online content quality, and mobile experience.

7. **Conference Hosting:** IEH's active conference program (referenced via ieh-conferences.com) indicates engagement with the academic ophthalmology community.
