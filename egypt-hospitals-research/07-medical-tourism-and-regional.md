## 1. Hospitals Co Egypt

### Overview

Hospitals Co Egypt is a Cairo-based medical tourism facilitation company that connects international patients with a wide network of Egyptian healthcare providers. The company positions itself as a comprehensive medical tourism gateway, offering end-to-end patient coordination from initial consultation through post-treatment follow-up. Egypt saw a 200% increase in medical tourism in 2024, and facilitators like Hospitals Co Egypt play a central role in channeling that demand.

- **Type:** Medical Tourism Facilitator
- **Headquarters:** Cairo, Egypt
- **Website:** [hospitalscoegypt.com](https://hospitalscoegypt.com)

### Services & Specialties

**Hospital Network:**
- 20 Class A hospitals (high-tech, specialized care)
- 30 Class B hospitals (mid-level, broad specialty coverage)
- 50 Class C hospitals (affordable general care)
- 500+ associated laboratories

**Core Specialties:**
- Oncology excellence programs
- Cardiac surgery (open heart, catheterization)
- Orthopedic surgery (joint replacement, sports medicine)
- Bariatric surgery
- Cosmetic and plastic surgery
- General and laparoscopic surgery
- Organ transplantation coordination
- Fertility and IVF treatments

**Patient Journey (7-Step Process):**
1. **Send Reports** -- Patient submits medical records remotely
2. **Team Review** -- Medical team evaluates and recommends treatment plan
3. **Cost Estimation** -- Detailed financial proposal provided before travel
4. **Travel Preparation** -- Visa assistance, flight coordination
5. **Treatment** -- Hospital admission and medical procedures
6. **Entertainment/Tours** -- Post-recovery archaeological and cultural tours
7. **Return Home** -- Post-treatment follow-up coordination

**Support Services:**
- Medical translation and interpretation
- Airport pickup and ground transportation
- Accommodation arrangement (patient and companions)
- 24/7 patient coordinator during stay
- Post-treatment follow-up via telemedicine

### Website Sections & Features

| Section | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Home | Hero banner, value proposition, trust indicators |
| About Us | Company mission, medical tourism in Egypt overview |
| Services | Treatment categories with descriptions |
| Hospitals | Network hospital profiles (Class A/B/C) |
| Patient Journey | Visual 7-step process flow |
| Medical Tourism | Egypt as a medical destination (cost advantages, quality) |
| Contact | Inquiry form, WhatsApp, phone |

### Fee Structure

No fee information is published on the website. Costs are provided on a case-by-case basis after medical record review (Step 3 of patient journey). The company operates as a facilitator and likely earns commission from network hospitals.

### Contact Information

- **Website:** [hospitalscoegypt.com](https://hospitalscoegypt.com)
- **Location:** Cairo, Egypt
- **Contact Method:** Website inquiry form

### Analysis

Hospitals Co Egypt exemplifies the growing medical tourism facilitation sector in Egypt. Its 100+ hospital network spanning all three classification tiers gives it flexibility to match patients across budget levels. The integrated tourism component (recovery + archaeological tours) is a unique selling proposition specific to Egypt. However, the lack of transparent pricing and limited online reviews makes it difficult to assess patient satisfaction or compare costs. The Egyptian government's new "Tour n' Cure" digital platform may increase competition in this space.

### Key Observations

1. **Digital Gap is Massive:** Of the 15 hospitals/facilitators studied, only 5 have websites that meet basic 2026 standards. Three major tourist-area hospitals (South Sinai, Pyramids Sharm, Luxor International) have no website at all. This represents both a market failure and a business opportunity.

2. **Resort-Area Hospitals Underinvest in Digital:** Hospitals in Hurghada, Sharm El Sheikh, and Luxor -- cities that depend on international visitors -- have the worst digital presence. This contradicts the logic that tourist-facing businesses should be the most digitally accessible.

3. **Medical Tourism is Organizing:** The proliferation of facilitation platforms (Hospitals Co Egypt, EgyHealth Gate, Tour n' Cure, Care N' Tour, PyraMedicine, Lotus, TMC) indicates that medical tourism in Egypt is transitioning from informal to organized. The government's "Tour n' Cure" platform could be transformative.

4. **Accreditation as Differentiator:** Elaraby Hospital's triple accreditation (JCI + GAHAR + ISO 22000) is exceptional. In a market where most hospitals cannot confirm any international accreditation, verified quality credentials are a significant competitive advantage.

5. **Psychiatric Care Gap:** Behman Hospital is the only dedicated psychiatric facility found in this research. Given the WHO's estimates of mental health burden in the MENA region, this suggests either an underserved market or that psychiatric care is primarily delivered through general hospital psychiatry departments.

6. **Alexandria's Smouha Corridor:** Three private hospitals (SIH, Andalusia Smouha, Alexandria International) are clustered within the Smouha district on/near Bahaa El Din El Ghatwary Street, creating a de facto healthcare corridor in Alexandria.

7. **New Cairo Growth:** Nasaaem Hospital's 5th Settlement location reflects the broader trend of premium healthcare following population growth to New Cairo and East Cairo suburbs.
