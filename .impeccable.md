# Al Noor Hospital — Project Impeccable

## Design Context

### Users
- **Primary**: Egyptian general public — patients, their families, and community members seeking healthcare services
- **Secondary**: Job seekers, researchers, and media interested in the hospital's achievements
- **Context**: Users may be anxious, in health distress, or searching for a trusted institution — they need clarity, quick scanning, and a sense of confidence
- **Job to be done**: Evaluate the hospital's credibility, find departments/doctors, understand services, and get emergency/contact information — often on mobile, often under stress

### Brand Personality
- **Voice**: Authoritative but warm, formal but approachable — a respected public institution, not a glossy private clinic
- **Tone**: Calm, trustworthy, professional — communicates competence without coldness
- **3-word personality**: Trustworthy · Warm · Dignified
- **Emotional goal**: The visitor should feel "this is a hospital I can trust with my family"

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Professional healthcare with a subtle premium feel — think Mayo Clinic meets a modern Middle Eastern public institution
- **Reference feel**: Clean white surfaces with deep navy authority and warm gold accents — dignified without being stuffy, modern without being flashy
- **Light mode only** (current implementation) — appropriate for healthcare readability
- **Anti-references**: No neon colors, no playful animations, no "startup" feel, no sterile plainness

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over decoration** — every design decision must help a stressed user find information faster
2. **Trust signals are visual** — awards, credentials, years of service, and staff count should be prominently displayed, not hidden
3. **Warm authority** — navy says competence; gold says humanity and excellence; together they say "trusted institution"
4. **Mobile-first urgency** — emergency numbers and CTAs must be reachable in ≤2 taps from any state
5. **Bilingual dignity** — both Arabic and English deserve full visual treatment; Arabic is not a secondary or smaller version
